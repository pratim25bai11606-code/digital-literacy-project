# ***Case Study: Phishing Attack***



##### **Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers impersonate a trusted entity (such as a bank, company, or government organization) to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information like passwords, OTPs, or financial details.**

##### 

##### **A typical phishing attack occurs in several steps. First, the attacker creates a fake email, message, or website that looks legitimate. Next, the victim receives this communication, often marked as “urgent” (e.g., account verification or payment issue). The message usually contains a link that redirects the user to a fake website resembling the original. When the victim enters their login or banking details, the attacker captures the information. Finally, the stolen data is used for unauthorized transactions or identity theft.**

##### 

##### **A common target group includes students, working professionals, and elderly users—especially those who may not verify links carefully or are less aware of cyber threats.**

##### 

##### **For example, a student receives an email claiming to be from their bank, asking to update KYC details. They click the link, enter credentials, and within minutes, money is withdrawn from their account.**

##### 

##### **The consequences of phishing include financial loss, identity theft, emotional stress, and compromised personal data. In severe cases, it may also affect credit scores or lead to long-term fraud.**

##### 

##### **This case highlights the importance of awareness, verifying sources, and avoiding suspicious links to stay safe online.**
